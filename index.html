Bay Management

20 bays, always accounted for — real-time status, type profiles, and forward occupancy projection.

⬡
Bay Management — 20 Bays, Always Accounted For
The bay board is the heartbeat of the entire system. Every bay carries a profile — chassis lift, drive-on lift, alignment rack, lube pit, tire mount — and whether it's currently occupied, reserved, cleaning, or open. The key intelligence is the daily occupancy projection: at any moment the system reads every appointment and projects forward hour by hour to show exactly which bays are spoken for and when they free up. If a customer calls at 10 AM asking for an alignment, it checks whether Bay 7 is clear in the 11 AM–12:30 PM window needed — preventing double-booking at the resource level, not just the calendar level. Bay types are granular: a chassis lift and drive-on lift are not interchangeable. Some bays support dual-purpose capability tags.

Appointments & Waitlist

Full booking lifecycle — from online request through bay assignment and completion.

◷
Appointments on Book — Full Lifecycle System
Every appointment carries: customer identity, vehicle (VIN-linked for history), requested service, preferred tech, scheduled window, bay-type requirement, and parts pre-order status. The waitlist is a live priority queue ordered by arrival time, urgency, and service type. The system matches each appointment to the optimal bay + tech + parts bundle simultaneously — not just a calendar slot. When a job runs long, the ripple recalculates downstream automatically. Technicians see their personal queue with bay, car, service, and parts staging status. The no-show predictor scores every appointment in the next 48 hours and fires targeted reminders to high-risk slots, reducing no-shows by 25–35%.

Walk-in Intake

Smart bay assignment, real-time wait-time calculation, and automated customer notification.

↪
Walk-in Accommodation — The Hardest Problem, Elegantly Solved
Walk-ins are the chaos variable. The system handles them with three-step intelligence: (1) scans all bays for current and near-term openings based on active work orders and appointments, (2) calculates a realistic wait-time estimate based on the service type and available bay, and (3) presents options to the advisor: take the walk-in now, add to waitlist with a time estimate, or offer the next open appointment slot. As soon as a bay of the right type clears, the system auto-triggers an SMS or email to the walk-in customer — "your bay is ready, arrive within 15 minutes." If they don't respond, the slot rolls to the next customer automatically.

Work Orders

Every car's visit tracked from intake to invoice — role-differentiated views for customer, tech, and manager.

◫
Work Orders — The Spine of Everything
A work order opens the moment an advisor creates it and doesn't close until the vehicle is out the door. It carries: customer and vehicle info, bay assignment, assigned tech(s), service line items (each with labor code, estimated and actual time, parts consumed), a live status progression (Waiting → In Bay → Diagnosis → In Progress → QC → Ready → Closed), tech notes, manager notes, and photo documentation. Critically, the work order presents differently by role — the customer sees a clean status tracker, the tech sees labor codes and a running timer, the advisor sees payment status, and the manager sees all open orders simultaneously with alerts for any job running past book time.

Roles & Permissions

Five differentiated role views — each person sees exactly what they need, nothing more.

◈
Role-Differentiated Access — Right Information, Right Person
Work orders and bay data present differently by role. The Customer sees a clean status tracker (like a UPS tracking page) via an SMS link — no account required. The Technician sees labor codes, parts needed, a timer running against book time, and dictation input for findings. The Service Advisor sees the full order with customer communication history and upsell recommendations. The Store Manager sees all open work orders across all bays simultaneously with alerts for anything running past book time. The Parts Associate sees the staging queue and incoming reorder alerts.

Services & Inventory

Service catalog with bay and tech requirements, book time, and real-time parts on-hand tracking.

◆
Services & Inventory — Tied Together at the Line Item
Every service in the catalog carries: required bay type(s), required tech skill level, default book time, flagged parts with standard quantities, and price. When a service is added to a work order, it auto-suggests the necessary parts from inventory and alerts the parts associate to stage them. If a part is at or below the reorder threshold, it flags immediately — before the appointment, not during the job. Inventory tracks on-hand quantity, reorder point, preferred vendor, and cost. Parts consumption is deducted automatically when a work order closes. Integration with AutoZone Pro, NAPA, and O'Reilly provides real-time availability lookup and same-day ordering directly from the work order screen.

Notifications Engine

Event-driven alerts for customers and technicians — SMS, email, and push at every critical touchpoint.

◉
Closing the Loop on Every Touchpoint
The notification engine is fully event-driven: every state change in a work order or bay fires the appropriate rule automatically. For customers: appointment confirmation (instant), reminder (24hr and 2hr prior), bay-ready alert, service-started notification, service-completed with invoice preview, and post-visit satisfaction survey. For technicians: new car assigned, customer checked in, car in bay, parts staged, manager notes added. Templates are per-service-type so tone and content are relevant. Twilio handles SMS; SendGrid handles email. Zero manual steps — the engine fires on its own.

Store Hours of Operation

Hours drive every scheduling rule — all booking, bay availability, and staffing is bounded by this config.

◑
Store Hours — The Foundation of All Scheduling
Store hours drive every scheduling rule: no appointments outside operating hours, walk-in intake ends 90 minutes before close, bay turnover cleaning is blocked against the last slot of the day. For a national chain, the system supports per-location hour configurations, holiday schedules, and seasonal hours — managed centrally with local overrides. Hours changes propagate instantly to all booking channels (website, phone, app) and to all scheduling algorithms. The system automatically blocks appointment booking windows when hours are reduced and notifies affected customers with reschedule options.

AI Agents

12 specialized agents — predictive, autonomous, generative, and real-time — operating across the entire shop.

✦
AI + Agentic Intelligence — The Unbeatable Competitive Moat
Every other shop management system records what happened. ShopFlow's AI acts on what's about to happen — before the bay goes idle, before the part runs out, before the customer no-shows, before the tech falls behind book time. At national scale, the network effect compounds: every location feeds the same training pipeline, patterns discovered at one store propagate to all stores automatically. The shift from reactive to proactive is the entire competitive moat. The agents operate in four layers: Predictive (see what's coming), Autonomous (act without human steps), Generative (language and documentation), and Real-time (optimize the live floor).

Predictive

Autonomous
